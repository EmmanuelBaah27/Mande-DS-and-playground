**INTRODUCTION**

* **3 myths about careers**   
  * Your career should be connected to your degree: Today’s careers do not care about your degree. The exception is the case of trained/licensed professionals. It’s rather about having in-demand skills the recruiters are willing to hire you for.   
  * You have to be 100% sure about what you want to do and it is a straight path from there: The path is never straight and will be full of uncertainties. You can’t think your way to clarity. It comes  with motion and you can always switch paths at any time. It is never too late  
  * Follow your passion: There is nothing wrong about doing what you love. But for it to be sustainable, someone has to pay you for it and it should connect to a real world problem. Monetizing your passion should be the focus.

**DISCOVERING YOUR OPTIONS**

* **Factors that help you find clarity on what to do: PIVOTS**

	**Tasks**

* Accept 10-day self-discovery career clarity challenge  
  * Take a work preference test to discover the work preference bucket you fit into. This includes Focuser (self starter), Relator (enthusiastic), Integrator (Finisher), Operator (Detailer)  
  * Take MBTI test to map out your personality—strengths and areas of improvement  
  * Take Holland test to map out interests  
  * Indicate preferred industries and industries you dislike  
  * Share hobbies and things users obsess about to point to self reported interests  
  * Take values test to discover what you will not compromise on and constraints  
  * Complete opportunities and threats assessment which directly relates to your environment. This includes where you want to work (up to 5 countries), work type (remote, in-office, hybrid), comfortability with career risks (1 to 5 with 1 being the least), openness to learning new skills, willingness to invest financially in their career etc  
  * Complete a skills audit. This includes a reflection or upload of resume, portfolio, LinkedIn profile pdf and any career document to extract skills and knowledge. They will also provide details on certifications and training programs, university course, certifications etc.
