## What it is

Mande is a career operating system for young African professionals. It combines a guided career clarity curriculum, a daily career journal, and AI-powered insights into a single companion that helps students decide what to build, capture what they're doing, and move their career forward with intention. The promise: less time overwhelmed by career decisions, more time making deliberate progress.

---

## Who it's for

**Primary user:** Early-career professionals, 21–27, in Africa — starting with Ghana and Nigeria. Often 2nd/3rd-year university students or recent graduates navigating high-performance industries (tech first). Their reality: busy delivering work, unsure how to manage their career alongside it, facing uneven access to networks and structured guidance, and making first-generation-professional decisions.

**Secondary user:** Career transitioners, 23–33, changing paths into high-performance industries. Often come to Mande already employed but seeking clarity on the next move.

**Sponsor (adjacent, not primary):** Family members, mentors, or scholarship schemes who pay for students via the Pay-for-me flow. Their view is limited — progress and milestones, not raw content.

---

## Core user goals

1. **Find career clarity** — move from "I don't know what to do" to a chosen path via the 10-day career clarity challenge.
2. **Track daily work and learning** — capture wins, tasks, learnings, setbacks, feedback, and decisions as they happen, so the evidence exists when it's needed.
3. **Get AI-powered insights and recommendations** — surface patterns across scattered entries and convert them into actionable next steps.
4. **Navigate networking and informational interviews** — find the right people, craft outreach, and turn conversations into agency (the CIA loop: Curiosity → Insights → Agency).
5. **Prepare for applications** — resumes, cover letters, LinkedIn, interview answers — pulled from the journal, not built from scratch.

## Design principles

- **Warm, witty, real voice.** "Female Trevor Noah" — brilliant best friend energy. Never preachy, never robotic, never distant.
- **Guide discovery, don't deliver answers.** Probing questions first; explanations only when needed. Help students think, not hand them conclusions.
- **Clarity comes with motion.** Students won't think their way to certainty. Every screen should invite action, not another decision.
- **Culturally grounded, never flattening.** African identity is not a monolith. No Silicon Valley archetypes as universal truth. Locally resonant examples.
- **Student-led success.** Success is defined on the student's terms — stable government job, entrepreneurship, or leaving for opportunity elsewhere all valid.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Anchor every concept to relatable examples from the student's lived reality.
- **One question at a time.** Never layer questions. Momentum over thoroughness in a single turn.

## Current focus (Q1 2026)

Three parallel OKRs driving the quarter:

1. **Validate curriculum delivery infrastructure and path to monetization** — AI chat workflow delivering career clarity curriculum to 100 students (25 free, 75 paid), 70% completion target; payment integrations + Pay-for-me fixed fee ($15–$20); lean sponsor portal with 80% signup rate.
2. **Launch mobile app + notifications for retention** — lean mobile (login, dashboard, chat UI); 70% download rate among signups; One Signal notifications; 50% WAU target.
3. **Grow socials + WhatsApp community** — 60% social growth across IG/Twitter/LinkedIn; 200+ signups with 50% in 2nd/3rd year; 50% scholarship students; 50% WhatsApp engagement rate.

The through-line: prove that a chat-based, Duolingo-style curriculum delivery beats static content, and that Pay-for-me unlocks a market that "pay yourself" does not.

## What Mande is not

- **Not a job board.** We don't list openings or match CVs to roles.
- **Not a generic chatbot.** Scope is career navigation. Off-topic questions get acknowledged and redirected, not answered at length.
- **Not a Western-centric career coach.** Linear-progression, individual-achievement frameworks are explicitly challenged.
- **Not a static content library.** Curriculum is delivered conversationally, paced, and evaluated — not linked as articles to read.
- **Not a replacement for licensed professionals.** Legal, financial, psychological, medical questions get a redirect to a qualified professional.
- **Not a grade-school LMS.** No coursework grades, no pass/fail. Evaluation is formative — sharpening the student's reasoning, not ranking them.
