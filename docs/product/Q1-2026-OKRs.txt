# Assumption tracking 

During the meeting, several key assumptions were identified as the foundation for the Q1 2026 OKRs. These assumptions are critical to track because they will determine whether the strategy is validated or needs to be disproved.

Below are the refined assumptions, organized under their respective OKRs and key results:

**OKR 1: Validate curriculum delivery infrastructure and test path to monetization**

**Key Result:** Launch AI-powered workflow for curriculum delivery (100 students); target 70%+ completion rate.

* Engagement Model: A chat-based interface is more effective than a static UI for delivering curriculum to students.  
* Behavioral Triggers: Using "Duolingo-like" principles (notifications that trigger user re-engagement) will prevent users from skipping steps and drive higher completion rates than self-navigation.

**Key Result:** Implement payment integrations and "Pay-for-me" feature; achieve 90% activation rate.

* Payment Preference: Students generally prefer not to pay for services themselves, creating a need for third-party payment options.  
* Distribution Nodes: Students can act as distribution nodes for revenue by sharing payment links with their personal networks.  
* Activation vs. Payment: A high (90%) activation rate is realistic because students will prioritize seeking external funding over paying out of pocket.  
* However, it is also assumed that students who do not expect anyone to pay for them may fail to activate the feature entirely  
* Price Sensitivity: A fixed fee of $15–$20, based on estimated token usage, is a viable market rate for the career clarity curriculum. We will review when we discover more

**Key Result:** Implement lean version of sponsor portal; achieve 80% signup rate.

* Investor Mindset: Providing visibility into student progress will shift the sponsor's mindset from "donation" to "investment"  
* Retention of Sponsors: Access to real-time milestones through a portal will encourage repeat sponsorships  
* It is noted that for individual sponsors (like family members), repeat behavior is contingent on them having other relatives in similar student scenarios.

**OKR 2: Launch mobile app and a strong notifications system to drive retention**

**Key Result:** Achieve a 70% download rate among signed-up users.

* App Superiority: Mobile app notifications are significantly more effective for driving retention than community-based engagement (WhatsApp) or emails.  
* Mandatory Transition: Forcing users to download the app to access the curriculum from the web portal is an effective way to drive adoption, despite the risk of user churn.

**Key Result:** Test out notifications; target 50% Weekly Active Users (WAU).

* Personalization over Generics: Personalized, targeted notifications (e.g., reminding a user exactly where they left off in a chat) are required to drive engagement, as generic calendar invites or group messages often fail (Mande Journal experience).  
* Engineering Prioritization: If engineering time is constrained, focusing exclusively on the mobile app's chat interface is a better investment than maintaining a web-based chat.

**OKR 3: Build socials and funnel students into the WhatsApp community**

**Key Result:** Grow signups to 200+ students (50% in 2nd/3rd year).

* The "Sweet Spot": Second and third-year students are the ideal target demographic because they are actively exploring careers, whereas first-years are focused on grades and fourth-years are in an "accelerated" panic for immediate jobs.

**Key Result:** Improve the fraction of scholarship students to 50% of signups.

* B2B Strategy Validation: Intentionally recruiting scholarship students provides the necessary data and metrics to fortify a B2B strategy with scholarship schemes and foundations.

**Key Result:** Maintain at least 50% engagement rate within the WhatsApp community.

* Engagement Definition: Surface-level interactions, such as responding to polls or simply saying "hi," are valid indicators of initial community health and participation. Tracking engagement here is aimed at precision and not accuracy

**Analogy for Understanding:**  
Think of these assumptions as the scaffolding for a new building. The OKRs are the floor plans, but the scaffolding (assumptions) is what holds everything in place while the structure is being built. If one piece of scaffolding proves weak—such as students not wanting to use the "Pay-for-me" link—the team must adjust the support structure to ensure the whole building doesn't lean in the wrong direction.
